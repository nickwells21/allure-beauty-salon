# Allure Booth Rental Tenant-Acquisition (Condensed)

## Goal
Generate high-intent inquiries from **experienced hair stylists** who want to start/expand their own booth-rental business (not commission-based, not appointment marketing).

## Most effective acquisition approach (what to copy from the best operators)
1. **Single-purpose landing page + single conversion CTA**
   - The page’s job is to get visitors to **Apply / request availability**.
   - Reduce friction with a **multi-step or progressive form** (collect essentials first; ask deeper questions later).
2. **SEO targeted to “tenant intent” keywords**
   - Optimize for what stylists search when they’re looking for workspace, not what customers search for.
   - Core keyword pattern: `booth rental + city` / `salon suite rental + city` / `beauty suite for rent + city`.
3. **Local discovery optimization**
   - Keep **NAP** consistent (Name/Address/Phone) across your website and listings.
   - Use a correct **primary Google Business Profile category**.
   - Publish steady **photos/videos/posts** and pursue reviews/testimonials.
   - Add **LocalBusiness JSON-LD** to help search engines understand the entity.
4. **Recruiting is community-driven**
   - Most vacancies fill via **tenant referrals**, **tours/open houses**, and **social proof** from current tenants.
   - “Come see the space” events outperform cold marketing because stylists can evaluate the work environment in person.
5. **Incentives/events increase inquiry volume**
   - Move-in incentives and referral credits are common and typically lift response rates.
   - Open houses are usually structured as networking + guided tours + low-pressure conversion.

## What to build for Allure (Evansville, IN)
### Website funnel (one job per page)
- `Booth Rental / Salon Suite Rental in Evansville, IN` (primary)
- `Why Stylists Choose Allure` (independence, privacy, business-ready environment)
- `Apply to Rent a Booth` (conversion page)

### Apply form (progressive / multi-step)
- **Step 1 (required):** name, email, phone, license type, years of experience, specialty
- **Step 2 (required):** current booking model (own clients or renting), expected move-in date, optional/conditional portfolio link
- **Step 3 (conditional):** references / permission to contact (only if you genuinely verify)

### Tenant value messaging pillars
- Independence: keep earnings (no commission-based system)
- Workspace quality: turnkey, professional, privacy-forward
- Clarity: move-in requirements, licensing expectations, policies (reduce “uncertainty” friction)

## Off-site + conversion boosters
- **Referral program** for qualified stylists (rent credit/bonus structure)
- **Scheduled tours/open houses** (capture RSVPs as inquiries)
- **Listing placements** and industry communities where stylists browse suite/booth opportunities
- **Local partnerships** with cosmetology/training/certification programs (target experienced pros transitioning)
- Ongoing **tenant proof** (testimonials, day-in-the-life, real work, suite setup wins)

## Measurement (so you can improve weekly)
- Landing page CTR
- Application start rate
- Application completion rate
- Qualified vs unqualified inquiries (simple scoring rubric)
- Time-to-contact (how fast you follow up)
- Track by channel with UTM parameters
